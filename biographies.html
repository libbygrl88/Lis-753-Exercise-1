" Hurrem Sultan"

The first women recognized as being part of the sultanate of women was Hurren Sultan (b.?-1558). Widely believed to be from the Ukraine Hurrem Sultans was originally known as Alexandra. After she became a favorite of Sultan Suleiman the Magnificent he gave her the name Hurrem which means ''the cheerful one''. Hurrems influence over Suleiman grew and he broke many conventions for her. First was Hurrem baring Suleiman four sons when the custom was only allowing a concubine to bare one son to the sultan. Then Suleiman made the shocking decision to marry Hurrem and created the title 'Haseki Sultan'or 'chief consort'for her

"Mirimah Sultan"

Part of Hurrem Sultans legacy is seen in her daughter Mirimah (1522-1575), whose name means ''sun and moon''. Considered a power figure in the Sulanate of Women in her own right. Regarded as THE most powerful and influential of Ottoman princesses Mirimad carved a role for herself first as an advisor to her father, Suleiman the Magnificent, but also played a role in supporting her brother Sultan Selim ii.

"Nurbanu Sultan"

Another notable woman in the Sultanate of women was Nurbanu. Born Cecila Venier Baffo and the illegimate daughter of two ventian nobles Nurbanu was abducted and brought to Turkey as a young girl. Nurbanu became the favorite of Hurrems second son, Sezhade Selim. Like Hurrem before her Nurbanu became Selim's legal wife and granted the title of Haskei Sultan. During their years together Selim and Nurbanu had four daughters and one son, the future Murad the Third.
